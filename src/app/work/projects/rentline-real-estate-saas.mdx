## Overview

**Rentline** is a SaaS product I founded and actively develop. It is designed to modernize real estate management for landlords and property managers in Brazil.  
Inspired by platforms like *Landlord Studio*, Rentline goes further by combining a sleek, modern interface with localized features tailored to the Brazilian market.  

The platform simplifies property management, financial tracking, and tenant communication—making it the most complete and accessible solution of its kind in the region.

---

## Key Features

- **Property & Tenant Management**: Easily manage multiple units, track leases, and organize tenant details in one place.  
- **Automated Finance Tracking**: Built-in rent collection logs, expense tracking, and financial reporting designed for Brazilian regulations.  
- **Modern UI/UX**: A responsive, mobile-friendly dashboard crafted for clarity and speed.  
- **Scalable SaaS Infrastructure**: Built to grow with customers, from solo landlords to professional property managers.  
- **On-Demand Integrations**: Future-ready integrations for payments, CRM, and AI-powered insights.  

---

## Technologies Used

- **Next.js & React** – For building a fast, modern, and maintainable frontend.  
- **Supabase & PostgreSQL** – Providing authentication, database, and scalable backend infrastructure.  
- **Tailwind CSS & Custom Design System** – For a clean and professional UI.  
- **Vercel** – For seamless deployment and scalability.  

---

## Challenges and Learnings

The biggest challenge was balancing **usability** with **feature depth**. Many legacy real estate tools overwhelm users with complexity, so Rentline was designed with a **minimalist but powerful workflow**.  

Another challenge was ensuring **local compliance**—especially with financial features aligned to Brazilian landlords and property managers. This required deep research and careful system design to stay simple while meeting requirements.  

I also learned how crucial it is to design SaaS infrastructure for **scalability from day one**, as property management inherently grows in data volume over time.
